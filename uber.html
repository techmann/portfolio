    • Hey, I’m Brian Eckmann and I want to join Uber’s Seattle design team.

    I challenged myself to design and prototype a new feature for Uber in 1 day.

    PITCH

    People are always looking for things to do.


    Let’s give users another reason to order an uber.

    USER RESEARCH

    I surveyed 15 people and asked if they’d use a location-based feature to give insight into where people are ubering to.


    Find out in real time:

  • Events (concerts, sporting events, parties)
  • Popular destinations (e.g. what bars are happening)
  • Crowded areas to avoid for a quiet experience
    • 15/15 said yes

    DATA

    Foursquare is already integrated into the Uber platform using Location Intelligence.

    Tap into Eventbrite's robust API to integrate event data with locations.

    Twitter has powerful Activity Location Metadata to filter out tweets by GeoCoordinates.

    DESIGNS

    ICON

    I wanted the icon to analogize with location, but it was more important to center around seeing so I chose the eye.

    CARDS

    I used a card-style design that would enable each section to horizontally scroll through the feature's content.

    INTERACTIONS

    Tap the eye to show recent Uber dropoffs

    Hold down on a location to view what's going on around there

    Select a card to view more and order an uber directly to the location

    NEXT STEPS

    If I were to continue working on this, I would do the following:


  • Validate Privacy policies about sharing anonymous trip data
  • Watch users play with the prototype and ask questions
  • Work with another designer to clean up the UI (e.g. remove the 'Where to?' and frequent locations in 'See' mode)
  • Build a more robust version of the prototype for user research testing
  • Integrate real data from Foursquare, Eventbrite, and Twitter to test the data connections
