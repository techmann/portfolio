    • Redesigning T-Mobile's Flagship App

    ROLE : Sr Designer (UX Team)

    More than 65 million T-Mobile customers rely on the mobile app for common tasks such as paying the bill, upgrading a phone, or chatting with support. The current ‘Magenta’ app is vastly outdated. It’s flooded with [non-native] webviews, small touch targets, and below average task success rates. The COO of T-Mobile asked Digital Leadership to design a 'kickass' app.

    Creating a design system

    One of the big focuses of the mobile app redesign was creating reusable components with Sketch Symbols to streamline the design process. We built out a library of Symbols for components such as form fields, headers, navigation, and confirmation screens. This way new screens can be created quickly and drive consistency across the app. Development can also benefit from being able to reuse code blocks for building out the UI.

    Store Locator

    My first design exercise was to rethink the experience around locating a T-Mobile store from a mobile device. Our focused point was an unathenticated user who just downloaded the app and wants to find a store nearby. I took inspiration from GMaps, Starbucks, Yelp, and others to strike a balance of what existing paradigms users were used to seeing and what was important for converting prospective customers. The biggest difference was that the result density on a map would be much less compared to a search for ‘restaurants’ on Yelp.

    User Research Approved

    T-Mobile has a dedicated User Research team to test new designs through a variety of methods including usability tests, tree tests, longitudinal studies, and surveys. It helped validate design decisions before anything went further down the buy-in chain with product and business owners.

    Hand-in-hand with Product

    The UX team works very closely with the Product team. Every feature on the app is assigned a Product Owner who defines the user goals, contraints, requirements, and gathers customer feedback. All the designs are vetted with the respected Product Owners to make sure we are meeting functionality requirements and improving (not just changing) the customer experience.
