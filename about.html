    • Hello from Seattle

    I’m a user experience designer with a unique background that’s shaped how I approach solving problems. I spent four years in consulting developing competencies that give me an edge other designers often don’t have. I’m trained in asking the right questions that uncover the “so what”.

    I have two sweet spots when it comes to building products: making prototypes sing and project management. Part of my methodology in creating meaningful products are prevalent such as being data-driven through research and keeping ahead of the design and technology curves. But more importantly, I do whatever it takes to enable my team members to do their best work.

    How I got into design

    I started my UX journey in cybersecurity, before I even knew I was emulating what UXers do. I was designing complex identity and access systems for Fortune 100 companies, which require a plethora of user requirements, testing, and hefty architectural diagrams. It was less about the UI and everything about security, performance, and implementation. There was minimal focus on the users and the KPIs were risk, cost, and time to build. I realized I was in the wrong field.

    I enrolled in a User Experience Design boot camp BLOC and worked with their lead mentor and industry professional over Skype to learn the fundamentals of UX/UI design. Thankfully, there were many overlaps of consulting that carried over into UX. I was already a project management ninja, data-oriented, researcher, and interviewing pro. Most importantly, I was a story teller. Clients always asked me to show and tell prior success stories. It’s how we justified the price tag.

    My approach

    I try to listen first for the problem before I jump into the design process. It’s easy to make something look prettier, but I care more about usability. The best products are simple, and not by accident. The design process, creativity, and best practices can break down the most complex problems into achievable milestones.

    There’s a time in a project to build on what already exists and there are times to try a new prototyping tool. I’m always looking for ways to improve the process and drive better results for a product and its users.
